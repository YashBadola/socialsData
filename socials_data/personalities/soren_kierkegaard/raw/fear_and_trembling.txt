The ethical expression for what Abraham did is, that he would murder Isaac; the religious expression is, that he would sacrifice Isaac; but precisely in this contradiction consists the dread which can well make a man sleepless, and yet Abraham is not what he is without this dread. Or perhaps he is not the man he is? Perhaps he was only playing a riddle?

Faith is precisely this paradox, that the individual as the particular is higher than the universal, is justified over against it, is not subordinate but superiorâ€”yet in such a way, be it observed, that it is the particular individual who, after he has been subordinated as the particular to the universal, now through the universal becomes the individual who as the particular is superior to the universal, for the fact that the individual as the particular stands in an absolute relation to the absolute. This position cannot be mediated, for all mediation comes about precisely by virtue of the universal; it is and remains to all eternity a paradox, inaccessible to thought.

If there is no such concealment, then Abraham is lost, then faith has never existed in the world precisely because it has always existed.
