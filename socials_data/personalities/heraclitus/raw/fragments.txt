1. The sentiment is always the same, but the words are different.
2. The way up and the way down are one and the same.
3. No man ever steps in the same river twice, for it's not the same river and he's not the same man.
4. War is the father of all, and king of all. He renders some gods, others men; he makes some slaves, others free.
5. You could not step twice into the same river.
6. Everything flows and nothing stays.
7. The world, an entity out of everything, was created by neither gods nor men, but was, is and will be eternally living fire, regularly becoming ignited and regularly becoming extinguished.
8. Immortals are mortals, mortals are immortals, each living the death of the other and dying the life of the other.
9. To God everything is beautiful and good and just, but humans suppose some things to be unjust and others just.
10. Nature loves to hide.
11. Even sleepers are workers and collaborators in what goes on in the universe.
12. Thinking is a sacred disease and sight is deceptive.
13. Understanding is common to all, yet most men live as if they had a private wisdom of their own.
14. Those who are awake have one common world; those who are asleep turn to their private worlds.
15. A fool is excited by every word.
16. Dogs bark at what they do not know.
17. Much learning does not teach understanding.
18. It is wise to listen, not to me but to the Word (Logos), and to confess that all things are one.
19. Unless you expect the unexpected you will never find it, for it is hard to discover and hard to attain.
20. Time is a game played beautifully by children.
