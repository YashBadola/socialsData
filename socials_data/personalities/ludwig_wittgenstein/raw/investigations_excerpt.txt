1. "Excalibur" is a sharp bright sword. "Excalibur" is a proper name.
2. But what does the word "meaning" mean? If it means what it stands for, then when the sword is broken, the meaning is broken. But the sword can be broken, and the meaning of the word "Excalibur" is not broken.
3. For a large class of cases—though not for all—in which we employ the word "meaning" it can be defined thus: the meaning of a word is its use in the language.
4. And the meaning of a name is sometimes explained by pointing to its bearer.
5. If a lion could talk, we could not understand him.
6. Philosophy is a battle against the bewitchment of our intelligence by means of language.
