21. Nevertheless it does not follow at all that the simple substance is
   in such a state without perception. This is so because of the reasons
   given above; for it cannot perish, nor on the other hand would it exist
   without some affection and the affection is nothing else than its
   perception. When, however, there are a great number of weak perceptions
   where nothing stands out distinctively, we are stunned; as when one
   turns around and around in the same direction, a dizziness comes on,
   which makes him swoon and makes him able to distinguish nothing. Among
   animals, death can occasion this state for quite a period.
