Fragment 1
Although this Logos is eternally valid, yet men are unable to understand it -- not only before hearing it, but even after they have heard it for the first time. That is to say, although all things come to pass in accordance with this Logos, men seem to be quite without any experience of it - at least if they are judged in the light of such words and deeds as I am here setting forth.

Fragment 2
We should let ourselves be guided by what is common to all. Yet, although the Logos is common to all, most men live as if each of them had a private intelligence of his own.

Fragment 10
Couples are wholes and not wholes, what agrees disagrees, the concordant is discordant. From all things one and from one all things.

Fragment 12
On those who step into the same rivers, different and again different waters flow.

Fragment 30
This world, which is the same for all, no one of gods or men has made. But it always was and will be: an ever-living Fire, with measures of it kindling, and measures going out.

Fragment 45
You will not find the boundaries of soul by travelling in any direction, so deep is the measure of it.

Fragment 49a
In the same river we both step and do not step, we are and are not.

Fragment 50
Listening not to me but to the Logos it is wise to agree that all things are one.

Fragment 51
They do not understand how that which differs with itself is in agreement: harmony consists of opposing tension, like that of the bow and the lyre.

Fragment 52
Time is a child playing a game of draughts; the kingship is in the hands of a child.

Fragment 53
War is the father of all and the king of all; and some he has made gods and some men, some bond and some free.

Fragment 54
Invisible harmony is better than visible.

Fragment 60
The way up and the way down is one and the same.

Fragment 62
Immortals are mortal, mortals are immortal, living their death, dying their life.

Fragment 67
God is day and night, winter and summer, war and peace, surfeit and hunger; but he takes various shapes, just as fire, when it is mingled with spices, is named according to the savor of each.

Fragment 80
We must know that war is common to all and strife is justice, and that all things come into being and pass away through strife.

Fragment 88
And it is the same thing in us that is quick and dead, awake and asleep, young and old; the former are shifted and become the latter, and the latter in turn are shifted and become the former.

Fragment 90
All things are an exchange for Fire, and Fire for all things, even as wares for gold and gold for wares.

Fragment 91
It is not possible to step twice into the same river.

Fragment 101
I searched into myself.

Fragment 102
To God all things are fair and good and right, but men hold some things wrong and some right.

Fragment 111
Disease makes health pleasant and good, hunger satiety, weariness rest.

Fragment 119
Man's character is his fate.

Fragment 123
Nature loves to hide.
