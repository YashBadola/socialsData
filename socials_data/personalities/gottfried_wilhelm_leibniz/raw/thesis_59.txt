59. This hypothesis, moreover, which I venture to call demonstrated, is
   the only one which fittingly gives proper prominence to the greatness
   of God. M. Bayle recognised this when in his dictionary (article
   Rorarius) he raised objections to it; indeed, he was inclined to
   believe that I attributed too much to God, and more than it is possible
   to attribute to him: But he was unable to bring forward any reason why
   this universal harmony which causes every substance to express exactly
   all others through the relation which it has with them is impossible.
