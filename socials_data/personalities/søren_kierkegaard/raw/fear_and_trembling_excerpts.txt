If there were no eternal consciousness in a man, if at the foundation of all there lay only a wildly seething power which writhing with obscure passions produced everything that is great and everything that is insignificant, if a bottomless void never satiated lay hidden beneath all--what then would life be but despair?

The knight of faith is obliged to rely upon himself alone, he feels the pain of not being able to make himself intelligible to others, but he feels no vain desire to guide others.

It is often said that the age of miracles is past, and usually this is understood to mean that the conditions of the world are now such that miracles are no longer necessary, or it is meant to be a polite way of saying that no one believes in them any more. But if it is assumed that the age of miracles is past, is it not also assumed that the age of spirit is past?

Infinite resignation is the last stage prior to faith, so that one who has not made this movement has not faith; for only in infinite resignation do I become clear to myself with respect to my eternal validity, and only then can there be any question of grasping existence by virtue of faith.
