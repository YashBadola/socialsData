1. No man ever steps in the same river twice, for it's not the same river and he's not the same man.
2. The way up and the way down are one and the same.
3. This world, which is the same for all, no one of gods or men has made. But it always was and will be: an ever-living fire, with measures of it kindling, and measures going out.
4. War is the father of all and the king of all; and some he has made gods and some men, some bond and some free.
5. Men are deceived in the recognition of things that are obvious.
6. Much learning does not teach understanding.
7. Listening not to me but to the Logos it is wise to agree that all things are one.
8. If you do not expect the unexpected, you will not find it; for it is hard to be sought out and difficult.
9. Nature loves to hide.
10. Character is destiny.
11. Even sleepers are workers and collaborators in what goes on in the universe.
12. The sun is new each day.
13. Eyes and ears are bad witnesses to men if they have barbarian souls.
14. It is better to hide ignorance, but it is hard to do this when we relax over wine.
15. Corpses are more fit to be thrown out than dung.
16. The waking have one common world, but the sleeping turn aside each into a world of his own.
17. Time is a game played beautifully by children.
18. Everything flows and nothing stays.
19. We must not act and speak like men asleep.
20. All things are an interchange for fire, and fire for all things, just like goods for gold and gold for goods.
