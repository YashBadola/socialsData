Diapsalmata

What is a poet? An unhappy man who in his heart harbors a deep anguish, but whose lips are so fashioned that the moans and cries which pass over them are transformed into ravishing music.

I do not care for anything. I do not care to ride, for the exercise is too violent. I do not care to walk, for it is too strenuous. I do not care to lie down, for I should remain there, and I do not care to do that.

My sorrow is my castle. As the eagle builds his nest on the mountain peaks, so I build my castle in the clouds of the mind.

Let others complain that the age is wicked; my complaint is that it is paltry; for it lacks passion. Men's thoughts are thin and flimsy like lace, they are themselves pitiable like the lacemakers. The thoughts of their hearts are too paltry to be sinful. For a worm it might be regarded as a sin to harbor such thoughts, but not for a man who is formed in the image of God. Their lusts are dull and sluggish, their passions sleepy. They do their duty, these shopkeeping souls, but they clip the coin a little...

The Rotation of Crops

People with experience maintain that proceeding from a basic principle is the supposed method of all science. I listen to them, and I am amazed at the problem. It is indeed true; I have no basic principle; I cannot have one, for if I did, I would have to regret it. The starting point of my whole philosophy is the feeling of boredom. Boredom is the root of all evil. It is very curious that boredom, which itself has such a calm and sedate nature, can have such a capacity to initiate motion. The effect that boredom has is absolutely magical, but this effect is one not of attraction but of repulsion.
