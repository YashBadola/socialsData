The Lantern in Daylight
Diogenes lit a lamp in broad daylight and said, as he went about, "I am looking for a human."

The Cup
One day, observing a child drinking out of his hands, he cast away the cup from his wallet with the words, "A child has beaten me in plainness of living."

Alexander the Great
When Alexander the Great came to visit him and asked if there was any boon he could grant him, Diogenes replied, "Stand out of my sunlight."

Plato's Man
Plato had defined Man as an animal, biped and featherless, and was applauded. Diogenes plucked a fowl and brought it into the lecture-room with the words, "Here is Plato's man." In consequence of which there was added to the definition, "having broad nails."

On Exile
When someone reminded him that the people of Sinope had sentenced him to exile, he said, "And I sentenced them to stay at home."

Living in a Jar
He would often say that the gods had given to men the means of living easily, but this had been put out of sight because we require honeyed cakes, unguents and the like. Hence to a man whose shoes were being put on by his servant, he said, "You have not attained to full felicity, unless he wipes your nose as well; and that will come to be, when you have lost the use of your hands."

Begging from Statues
He was once seen begging from a statue; and when asked why he did so, "To get practice in being refused," said he.

On Philosophy
Being asked what he had gained from philosophy, he replied, "This at least, if nothing else -- to be prepared for every fortune."

On Fate
He used to say that he opposed confidence to fortune, nature to law, and reason to passion.

The Slave Auction
When he was captured by pirates and put up for auction, he was asked what he could do. He replied, "Govern men." And he told the crier to announce that if anyone wanted to buy a master, here was his chance.

Entering a House
One day he entered the house of a spendthrift and read him a lecture. When the man said, "Diogenes, why are you so hard on me?" he replied, "Because your house is like a theatre: lots of people go in, but no one comes out any better."

Reason or a Halter
He used to say that for the conduct of life we need right reason or a halter.

Burying Him
When asked how he wished to be buried, he said, "Face downwards." When asked why, he said, "Because in a little while everything will be turned upside down." (Referring to the rise of Macedon).

On Being Struck
When someone struck him with a beam and then said, "Look out," Diogenes struck him with his staff and said, "Look out."

The Only Real Commonwealth
He said that the only true commonwealth was that which is as wide as the universe.
