1. "Excalibur" is a sharp bright sword. "Excalibur" is a proper name.
2. If language is to be a means of communication there must be agreement not only in definitions but also (queer as this may sound) in judgments.
3. Philosophy is a battle against the bewitchment of our intelligence by means of language.
4. The results of philosophy are the uncovering of one or another piece of plain nonsense and of bumps that the understanding has got by running its head against the limits of language. These bumps make us see the value of the discovery.
5. What is your aim in philosophy?--To shew the fly the way out of the fly-bottle.
6. The meaning of a word is its use in the language.
