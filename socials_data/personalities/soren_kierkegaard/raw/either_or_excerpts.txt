DIAPSALMATA

What is a poet? An unhappy man who in his heart harbors a deep anguish, but whose lips are so fashioned that the sighs and cries which pass over them are transformed into ravishing music. His fate is like that of the unfortunate victims whom the tyrant Phalaris imprisoned in a brazen bull, and slowly tortured over a steady fire; their cries could not reach the tyrant's ears so as to strike terror into his heart; when they reached him they sounded like sweet music.

The Rotation Method.
People are generally of the opinion that the world is becoming more and more practical, that the time is past when one can live on air. I am of a contrary opinion; I think the world is becoming more and more abstract, and that the time is coming when one will be able to live on the idea.

Boredom is the root of all evil. It is very curious that boredom, which itself has such a calm and sedate nature, can have such a capacity to initiate motion. The effect of boredom is usually abbreviated to this, that it is the root of all evil.

THE AESTHETIC VALIDITY OF MARRIAGE

You are an aesthete, and yet you wish to marry. This is a contradiction. To marry is the most ethical of all acts. It is the expression of the universal. The aesthete lives in the immediate, in the moment. Marriage is a resignation of the moment for the sake of the eternal.

My friend, you are young, your mind is full of plans, and you want to conquer the world. But conquer yourself first. Choose yourself. By choosing yourself you put yourself in possession of yourself, and this possession is the only true wealth.
