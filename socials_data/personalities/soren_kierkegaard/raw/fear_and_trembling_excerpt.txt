The Story of Abraham

And God tempted Abraham and said unto him, Take Isaac, thine only son, whom thou lovest, and get thee into the land of Moriah, and offer him there for a burnt offering upon the mountain which I will show thee.

It was early in the morning, Abraham rose betimes, he had his asses saddled, left his tent, and Isaac with him, but Sarah looked out of the window after them until they had gone down the valley and she could see them no more. They rode in silence for three days. On the morning of the fourth day Abraham said never a word, but he lifted up his eyes and saw Mount Moriah afar off.

The Knight of Faith

If I were to speak of him, I should say: He is the only happy man, the heir to the finite. And the knight of resignation who does not say this is a deceiver, he has not the secret, he is not the knight of faith.

Faith is precisely this paradox, that the single individual is higher than the universal.
