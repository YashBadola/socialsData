1. "Excalibur" is a proper name.
2. The meaning of a word is its use in the language.
3. Language is a labyrinth of paths. You approach from one side and know your way about; you approach the same place from another side and no longer know your way about.
4. Philosophy is a battle against the bewitchment of our intelligence by means of language.
5. The results of philosophy are the uncovering of one or another piece of plain nonsense and of bumps that the understanding has got by running its head against the limits of language. These bumps make us see the value of the discovery.
6. A picture held us captive. And we could not get outside it, for it lay in our language and language seemed to repeat it to us inexorably.
7. What is your aim in philosophy?--To shew the fly the way out of the fly-bottle.
8. If a lion could talk, we could not understand him.
9. Light dawns gradually over the whole.
10. Don't think, but look!
