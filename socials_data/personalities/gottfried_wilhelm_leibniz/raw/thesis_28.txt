28. Men act in like manner as animals, in so far as the sequence of
   their perceptions is determined only by the law of memory, resembling
   the empirical physicians who practice simply, without any theory, and
   we are empiricists in three-fourths of our actions. For instance, when
   we expect that there will be daylight tomorrow, we do so empirically,
   because it has always happened so up to the present time. It is only
   the astronomer who uses his reason in making such an affirmation.
