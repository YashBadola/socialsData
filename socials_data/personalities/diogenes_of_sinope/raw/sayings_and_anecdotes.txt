Diogenes lit a lamp in broad daylight and said, as he went about, "I am looking for a human."

When Alexander the Great stood opposite him and asked, "Ask of me whatever you wish," Diogenes replied, "Stand a little out of my sun."

Plato defined man as "a featherless biped" and was applauded. Diogenes plucked a chicken and brought it into the lecture hall, crying, "Behold! Plato's man!"

Seeing a child drinking from his hands, he threw away his cup, saying, "A child has beaten me in plainness of living."

When someone said to him, "The people of Sinope condemned you to banishment," he replied, "And I condemned them to remain in Sinope."

He used to beg of statues. When asked why, he said, "To get practice in being refused."

A rich man invited him to his house and warned him not to spit on the floor. Diogenes, clearing his throat, spat in the man's face, saying, "I could find no meaner place."

When asked how he wished to be buried, he said, "Face down." When asked why, he said, "Because in a little while everything will be turned upside down."

Seeing the son of a prostitute throwing stones at a crowd, he said, "Careful, boy, you might hit your father."

When he saw officials leading away a temple-robber, he said, "The big thieves are leading away the little thief."

When asked what was the most beautiful thing in the world, he replied, "Freedom of speech."

He was asked where he came from, and he said, "I am a citizen of the world (cosmopolites)."
