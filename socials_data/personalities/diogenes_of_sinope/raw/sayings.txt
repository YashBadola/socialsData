When Alexander the Great asked him if he wanted anything, he said, "Yes, stand out of my sunlight."

When asked where he came from, he said, "I am a citizen of the world."

He lit a lamp in broad daylight and said, as he went about, "I am looking for a man."

Someone took him into a magnificent house and warned him not to spit, whereupon having cleared his throat he spat into the man's face, being unable, he said, to find a meaner receptacle.

When asked what he had gained from philosophy, he replied, "This at least, if nothing else -- to be prepared for every fortune."

Seeing a child drinking from his hands, he threw away his cup and said, "A child has beaten me in plainness of living."

When asked what was the right time to marry, he replied, "For a young man not yet: for an old man never at all."

He was asked what was the most beautiful thing in the world, and replied, "Freedom of speech."
